Montana's Wild and Scenic Rivers

Montana has 388 miles of river throughout the state that are designated as "Wild and Scenic": The Nation's highest form of river protection.
"The National Wild and Scenic Rivers System was created by Congress in 1968 to preserve certain rivers with outstanding natural, cultural, and recreational values in a free-flowing condition for the enjoyment of present and future generations."(Rivers.gov)

Hypothesis: Montana rivers that contain a "wild and scenic" designated section contain less impoundments (dams) and less impaired, or less severely impaired water than rivers with no designated section.

Water quality for this project will be defined by the Montana 2020 Final Water Quality Integrated Report (deq.mt.gov)
